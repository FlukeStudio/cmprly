## What Makes Kit Different

Kit was founded in 2013 by Nathan Barry, a creator who built the tool he wished existed. Unlike Mailchimp (built for small businesses) or ActiveCampaign (built for sales teams), Kit was built for one audience: independent creators who want to build, grow, and monetize an email list.

This focus manifests in every design decision:

- **Tag-based segmentation** instead of list-based: a subscriber who joins three segments counts as one contact, not three. Mailchimp charges per-contact across audiences—Kit doesn't.
- **Text-focused emails**: Kit's templates are intentionally plain. The philosophy: plain-text emails feel personal, get higher open rates, and trigger fewer spam filters.
- **Built-in commerce**: Sell digital products (ebooks, courses, templates) directly from Kit—no Gumroad or Shopify needed.
- **Creator Network**: Kit's built-in recommendation engine lets other creators promote your newsletter. No other email platform has this.

## The Price Hike (September 2025)

Kit raised Creator from $15/month to $39/month at 1,000 subscribers in September 2025—**a 160% increase.** This generated significant backlash from existing users, with Trustpilot ratings dropping sharply.

**Who this affects**: new customers and those coming off legacy plans. Existing customers on grandfathered pricing were affected with 100–400% increases depending on subscriber count.

**The silver lining**: the price hike makes Kit's free plan even more valuable. At the old pricing, upgrading to Creator was a no-brainer at $15/month. At $39/month, staying on the free plan longer makes financial sense—and with 10,000 free subscribers, you can.

## Visual Automation Builder

Kit's automation builder is the standout feature. It uses a drag-and-drop canvas where you define:

- **Triggers**: subscriber joins a form, clicks a link, purchases a product, receives a tag
- **Actions**: send email, add tag, remove tag, move to sequence, wait
- **Conditions**: if subscriber has tag X, take path A; if not, take path B

The result is complex behavior-triggered sequences built without code. A typical welcome sequence takes 10–15 minutes to configure.

**Example automation**: New subscriber joins via "SEO Checklist" lead magnet → gets tagged "SEO-interested" → receives 3-email SEO nurture sequence → after sequence, gets tagged "nurtured" → moves to general newsletter list.

Compared to competitors: Kit's builder is significantly more intuitive than Mailchimp's Customer Journeys and more visual than Brevo's form-based workflow builder. Only ActiveCampaign's builder rivals Kit's, and ActiveCampaign's starts at $49/month.

## Deliverability

Kit consistently ranks at the top of independent deliverability benchmarks. Tests from EmailToolTester (2025) and internal Kit data report inbox placement rates of **91–99.8%** — ahead of Mailchimp (~86%) and Brevo (~88%).

Kit attributes its deliverability to three factors:
1. Text-focused emails (HTML-heavy emails trigger more spam filters)
2. Strict list hygiene enforcement (inactive subscribers are flagged)
3. Dedicated IP infrastructure on paid plans

For creators whose income depends on emails reaching inboxes, this is a meaningful advantage.

## Digital Product Sales (Built-in Commerce)

Kit is the only platform that lets you sell digital products natively—no separate store or checkout tool. You can sell:

- Ebooks and PDFs
- Online courses
- Templates and presets
- Paid newsletter subscriptions

**Commerce fees**: Kit charges 3.5% + $0.30 per transaction (for U.S. creators). This is comparable to Gumroad's 10% flat fee and Shopify's transaction fees—but Kit doesn't charge a monthly platform fee for commerce. It's included with every plan.

For a creator selling a $97 course, Kit's fee is ~$3.70. Gumroad takes $9.70. Over 100 sales, the difference is $600.

## Creator Network

Kit's Creator Network is a recommendation engine that connects Kit creators to each other's audiences. When someone subscribes to another creator's newsletter, they may see a recommendation for your newsletter—driving organic subscriber growth at no cost.

No other email platform has an equivalent. Mailchimp is moving toward recommendation features but hasn't launched them. Beehiiv has a referral program for existing subscribers but not cross-creator discovery.

## FAQ

### Is Kit's free plan really 10,000 subscribers?
**Yes.** The free Newsletter plan includes up to 10,000 subscribers, unlimited broadcasts, unlimited landing pages, and unlimited forms. The only limitation is one basic automation.

### Can I sell digital products on the free plan?
**Yes.** Commerce is included on all plans. Kit takes 3.5% + $0.30 per transaction.

### How does Kit's pricing compare to Mailchimp?
At small lists, Mailchimp's $13/mo Essentials plan is cheaper than Kit's $39/mo Creator. But Mailchimp's free tier caps at 500 contacts—Kit gives you 10,000 for free. At 10,000 subscribers, Kit Creator costs ~$139/mo while Mailchimp Standard costs ~$100/mo.

### Why did Kit raise prices so much in 2025?
Kit stated the increase aligned pricing with the value of creator-focused features (visual automations, commerce, Creator Network). The free plan remained unchanged at 10,000 subscribers.

### Does Kit have an affiliate program?
**Yes.** Kit's affiliate program pays **30% recurring lifetime commission**—one of the most generous in SaaS. Available through Impact or Kit's direct program.
