## Kit: Best Visual Automation for Creators

Kit's visual automation builder is the gold standard for intuitive, drag-and-drop email sequences. You define triggers (subscriber joins a form, clicks a link, buys a product) and actions (send email, add tag, move to sequence) on a clean canvas that requires zero coding knowledge.

> *"Kit's automation builder supports tag-based segmentation—a single subscriber can trigger different automations across multiple segments without you paying for them twice. Mailchimp's list-based model can charge for the same contact in multiple audiences."*

**Best for:** Welcome sequences, product launch funnels, content upgrade delivery, and subscriber re-engagement. Kit's tags and triggers are particularly well-suited to creator workflows where subscribers self-segment based on interest (e.g., different lead magnets map to different nurture sequences).

**Missing:** No site tracking, no lead scoring on the Creator plan, and no conditional branching that adapts mid-sequence based on subscriber behavior beyond basic click/open triggers.

## ActiveCampaign: The Automation Powerhouse

ActiveCampaign is not an email tool with automation features—it's an automation platform that happens to include email. Its feature set is in a different league than the other three tools on this list.

> *"ActiveCampaign supports conditional branching where a subscriber's path through an automation adapts in real time—if they open an email, they go one route; if they don't, they go another; if they visit a specific page on your site, the automation pivots again. Kit and Brevo do this with basic if/then logic. ActiveCampaign does it with predictive AI."*

**Key automation features exclusive to ActiveCampaign:**
- **Lead scoring:** Assign points to contacts based on behavior (email opens, page visits, form submissions). Automatically move high-scoring leads to a sales sequence.
- **Site tracking:** Trigger automations when a contact visits specific pages on your website—even if they haven't opened an email.
- **Predictive sending:** AI analyzes each contact's past engagement and sends emails at the time they're most likely to open.
- **Split automations:** A/B test entire automation paths—not just subject lines, but whether a 5-email vs 3-email nurture sequence converts better.
- **900+ pre-built recipes:** Automations for ecommerce, SaaS, professional services, and education—designed by experts and ready to import.

**The tradeoff:** Complexity. ActiveCampaign's interface has a steep learning curve. For a solopreneur who needs welcome + nurture + re-engagement sequences, it's overkill. For one who runs a 10-step sales funnel with behavioral triggers and lead scoring, it's unmatched.

**Pricing:** ActiveCampaign starts at $15/month but the automation depth described above requires the Plus plan at $49/month (1,000 contacts).

## Brevo: Best Automation-to-Cost Ratio

Brevo's automation builder is functional rather than beautiful—multi-step workflows configured through form fields rather than a visual canvas. But at $9/month for 5,000 emails with unlimited contacts, it delivers the best automation-per-dollar ratio of any platform on this list.

> *"Brevo's Starter plan at $9/month includes multi-step automation workflows, web and event tracking, and A/B testing. ActiveCampaign's equivalent automation depth requires the $49/month Plus plan. For a solopreneur running 3–5 core sequences with monthly sends under 5,000 emails, Brevo delivers comparable automation at 82% lower cost."*

**What Brevo's automation does well:**
- Welcome sequences triggered by list signup
- Re-engagement campaigns triggered by inactivity
- Abandoned cart recovery (ecommerce)
- Multi-channel automation: email + SMS in the same workflow

**What Brevo's automation lacks:**
- Visual drag-and-drop (uses form-based configuration)
- Lead scoring and predictive features
- Site tracking-triggered automations
- Conditional branching as deep as ActiveCampaign's

## Mailchimp: Automation Improved, Still Catching Up

Mailchimp's Customer Journey builder has significantly improved in recent years. It now supports multi-step automations with triggers based on email engagement, purchase behavior, and audience segments. For solopreneurs who need basic-to-intermediate automation as part of a broader marketing platform, Mailchimp delivers.

**Where Mailchimp falls short:** The strongest automation features are gated behind higher pricing tiers. Conditional branching and AI-powered send-time optimization require the Standard plan ($20/month) or above. And even at the Premium tier, Mailchimp's automation depth doesn't match ActiveCampaign's.

> *"Mailchimp's Standard plan at $20/month (500 contacts) supports automated Customer Journeys with behavioral triggers, but the strongest features—including predictive segmentation and multivariate testing—require the Premium plan at $350/month. For a solopreneur, ActiveCampaign Plus at $49/month delivers more automation power at a fraction of Mailchimp Premium's cost."*

## Real-World Automation Examples

**Simple welcome sequence (all 4 tools, under 10 minutes each):**
1. Subscriber signs up → Tag added → Welcome email #1 (deliver lead magnet)
2. Wait 2 days → Welcome email #2 (introduce your best content)
3. Wait 3 days → Welcome email #3 (soft pitch for product/newsletter)
4. If opened email #3 → Tag "engaged" → Move to nurture sequence

**Advanced behavioral sequence (ActiveCampaign only):**
1. Subscriber signs up → Welcome sequence starts
2. If they visit your pricing page (site tracking) → Skip remaining welcome emails → Move to sales sequence immediately
3. If they don't open any emails for 30 days → Re-engagement sequence starts
4. Lead score crosses threshold → Notify you (or your CRM) that this contact is sales-ready

## FAQ

### Do I need automation as a solopreneur with under 1,000 subscribers?
**Yes—but basic.** A 3-email welcome sequence and a simple re-engagement flow cover 80% of what you need. Kit's free plan or Brevo's Starter plan ($9/month) handle this. You don't need ActiveCampaign.

### Can I build the same automations on Brevo that I can on Kit?
**Functionally, yes.** Both support multi-step workflows with triggers and conditions. Kit's experience is more visual and intuitive. Brevo's is more form-based. For the same set of 3–5 core automations, both deliver.

### What's the biggest difference between ActiveCampaign and the others?
**Intent.** Kit, Brevo, and Mailchimp add automation to email marketing. ActiveCampaign builds email marketing around automation. It's a CRM with email capability, not an email tool with automation features.

### Which platform integrates best with other tools?
ActiveCampaign (900+ integrations), followed by Mailchimp (300+). Kit and Brevo are more focused—fewer integrations but deeper ones for creator and multi-channel workflows respectively.
