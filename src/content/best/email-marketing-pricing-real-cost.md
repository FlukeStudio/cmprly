## Three Pricing Models, Radically Different Costs

### Per-Subscriber (Kit, Mailchimp, MailerLite)
You pay based on total subscribers—**including unsubscribed and inactive contacts.** This model rewards small, engaged lists and punishes growth. It's the most predictable when you're small (<2,500 subs) and the most punishing when you're not.

> *"Kit charges per subscriber including unsubscribed contacts. A 10,000-person list where 30% are unsubscribed still costs $139/month under per-subscriber pricing. Brevo's send-volume model ignores the 3,000 unsubscribeds completely—you only pay for the 7,000 active subscribers you actually email."*

### Per-Contact (Mailchimp)
A variant of per-subscriber that counts the same contact across multiple audiences. If "Jane" is in your newsletter audience AND your course buyer audience, Mailchimp counts her twice. Kit's tag-based model counts her once.

### Per-Email-Sent (Brevo)
You pay for volume—not list size. A 50,000-person list you email twice a month costs the same as a 5,000-person list you email 20 times a month. **This model is the cheapest for large, infrequently-emailed lists—and it's why Brevo dominates the "best value" category once lists pass 2,500 subscribers.**

## FAQ

### Which platform has the most predictable pricing?
**Brevo.** Send-volume pricing means your costs scale linearly with how much you email, not how many people are on your list. No surprises when contacts accumulate.

### Why is ActiveCampaign so expensive?
ActiveCampaign isn't an email tool—it's a CRM + automation platform. Its pricing reflects enterprise-grade features (lead scoring, site tracking, predictive sending) that most solopreneurs don't need. If you do need them, it's worth it. If you don't, it's overkill.

### Can I avoid paying by staying on free plans?
**Yes—longer than you think.** Kit's 10,000-subscriber free plan handles a real newsletter business. Brevo's unlimited contacts with 300 emails/day covers small lists. The catch: you'll lack automations (Kit) or daily send volume (Brevo).

### What hidden cost catches most people?
**Counting unsubscribed contacts.** Kit and Mailchimp charge per total subscriber including those who never open. Clean your list every 90 days—remove contacts unengaged for 60+ days. This single habit can cut your bill 10–30%.

### Are annual plans worth it?
**Almost always.** Kit saves 16% (2 months free). Mailchimp saves 15% on 10,000+ contacts. Brevo doesn't discount annual billing but locks in current pricing—useful if you expect price increases.
