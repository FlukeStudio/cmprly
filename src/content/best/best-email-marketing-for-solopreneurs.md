## Kit: Best for Creators & Course Sellers

Kit was built by creators, for creators. Every feature—from tagging to automations to commerce—is designed around a solo operator building an audience.

**As of July 2026, Kit's free Newsletter plan supports up to 10,000 subscribers with unlimited broadcasts, landing pages, and forms—the most generous free tier in email marketing.** The limitation: only one basic automation. Upgrade to Creator ($39/mo for 1,000 subs, billed annually at $33/mo) to unlock unlimited visual automations, sequences, and integrations.

> *"Kit's free plan at 10,000 subscribers is 20x larger than Mailchimp's 500-contact free tier. For a solopreneur adding 100 subscribers per month, Mailchimp forces a paid upgrade in 5 months—Kit's free plan lasts over 8 years at the same growth rate."*

**Pros & Cons:**

| ✅ Pros | ❌ Cons |
|---------|---------|
| 10K free subscribers, most generous in industry | Expensive at scale ($139/mo for 10K subs) |
| Visual automation builder is best-in-class for creators | Templates are text-focused, not design-rich |
| Built-in digital product sales (courses, ebooks) | September 2025 price hike (Creator up 160%) |
| Tag-based segmentation (no double-charging) | No SMS marketing |
| Creator Network for organic list growth | Advanced reporting only on Pro ($79/mo+) |

## Brevo: Best Budget Option

Brevo's pricing model is its superpower: **you pay per email sent, not per subscriber stored.** For solopreneurs who email infrequently to a growing list, this is dramatically cheaper than Kit or Mailchimp.

**As of July 2026, Brevo's free plan offers 300 emails/day with unlimited contact storage.** The Starter plan at $9/month raises the ceiling to 5,000 emails per month—sufficient for a weekly newsletter to 1,250 subscribers.

> *"Brevo charges $9/month for 5,000 emails to unlimited contacts. Kit charges $39/month for 1,000 subscribers. For a creator with 2,500 subscribers sending twice a month, Brevo is approximately 77% cheaper on a per-month basis."*

Where Brevo shines: multi-channel marketing. Email, SMS, WhatsApp, and transactional emails all run from one dashboard at no extra platform cost.

**Pros & Cons:**

| ✅ Pros | ❌ Cons |
|---------|---------|
| Send-volume pricing = cheapest at scale | Templates limited (40+ vs Mailchimp's 100+) |
| Unlimited contacts on all plans | No digital product selling or creator tools |
| SMS + WhatsApp built in | Automation builder less visual than Kit's |
| GDPR-compliant, EU-hosted | Shared IP pool can affect deliverability |

## Mailchimp: Best All-in-One

Mailchimp is the most recognizable name in email marketing. Its integrated suite—email + landing pages + social ads + retargeting—eliminates tool sprawl for solopreneurs who want one platform to do everything.

**As of July 2026, Mailchimp's free plan supports 500 contacts with 1,000 monthly email sends (500/day cap).** Paid plans start at $13/mo (Essentials) and $20/mo (Standard, which adds automations). At scale, Mailchimp's per-contact pricing escalates significantly—25,000 contacts on Standard costs approximately $210/month.

> *"Mailchimp's free plan was reduced to 500 contacts with a 500-email daily cap—for a solopreneur adding 100 subscribers per month, that ceiling arrives in 5 months. Kit's free tier, at 10,000 subscribers, would accommodate 8 years of growth at the same pace."*

Where Mailchimp still wins: 100+ professional templates, AI content optimizer, and the most extensive integration library (300+ apps). For ecommerce solopreneurs running Shopify stores, Mailchimp's product recommendation and abandoned cart tools are competitive.

**Pros & Cons:**

| ✅ Pros | ❌ Cons |
|---------|---------|
| 100+ polished email templates | Free plan significantly reduced (500 contacts) |
| All-in-one: email + social + ads + landing pages | Pricing escalates steeply at scale |
| 300+ integrations, best ecosystem | Automation less intuitive than Kit |
| Shopify/WooCommerce ecommerce features | No native digital product sales |

## MailerLite: Best Simple Option

MailerLite offers a clean, intuitive experience at competitive pricing. Its free plan supports 500 subscribers and 12,000 monthly emails with automation included—generous compared to Mailchimp's restricted free tier.

Paid plans start at $10/month. The drag-and-drop editor is among the most intuitive in the category, and deliverability rates consistently rank near the top in industry benchmarks. For solopreneurs who want email marketing to just work without a steep learning curve, MailerLite is the natural choice.

**Limitations:** No built-in CRM, fewer integrations than Mailchimp, and the free plan was reduced from 1,000 to 500 subscribers in late 2025.

---

## Beehiiv: Best for Newsletter-First Creators

Beehiiv was built for one purpose: growing and monetizing a newsletter. It includes a built-in referral program, a native ad network, and paid newsletter subscriptions with 0% platform commission.

The free Launch plan covers 2,500 subscribers. The Scale plan at $42/month adds custom domains and advanced analytics. For a solo creator whose business *is* the newsletter, Beehiiv's growth tools are unmatched.

**Limitations:** Automation is basic compared to Kit or Brevo. Not suitable for traditional marketing funnels or non-newsletter use cases.

## When to Choose Each

- **Choose Kit if**: you're a content creator, course seller, or blogger who needs visual automations, wants to sell digital products, and starts with a small-to-medium list.
- **Choose Brevo if**: budget is your top priority, you anticipate rapid list growth, or you want multi-channel (email + SMS) in one platform.
- **Choose Mailchimp if**: you need email + social ads + retargeting under one roof and your list is small-to-medium (under 5,000 contacts).
- **Choose MailerLite if**: you want the simplest, cleanest email tool that handles the basics well without complexity.
- **Choose Beehiiv if**: your business *is* your newsletter—and you want built-in growth and monetization tools.

## FAQ

### Which platform is truly free for the longest time?
**Kit.** Its free plan supports up to 10,000 subscribers with unlimited broadcasts—you can run a real newsletter business for years without paying. Brevo's unlimited contacts with 300 emails/day is also strong. Mailchimp's 500-contact cap forces upgrades fastest.

### Can I switch platforms later without losing subscribers?
**Yes, but with friction.** All platforms support CSV export/import. Kit offers free concierge migration on higher plans. Migration always carries deliverability risk—your new platform's IP reputation may differ.

### Which has the best deliverability?
Independent benchmarks (EmailToolTester 2025) show Kit leading at 91–99.8% inbox placement, followed by Brevo (~88%) and Mailchimp (~86%). Kit's text-focused emails contribute to its strong deliverability scores.

### Do any of these let me sell digital products?
**Only Kit** has built-in digital product sales with no separate checkout tool needed. Beehiiv offers paid newsletter subscriptions. Mailchimp and Brevo focus on physical product ecommerce.

### What hidden costs should I watch for?
Kit charges per subscriber including unsubscribed contacts (clean your list regularly). Mailchimp charges for the same contact across multiple audiences. Brevo's shared IP pool on the free plan means your deliverability may be affected by other senders' behavior.
